The end of a year is a time for us to pause, reflect on our work and its impact, and prepare ourselves for what comes next.

We hope that we can translate all the work and learnings into activities and technology tools that can serve the sector better in the future. To all the NGOs that have been with us, providing guidance on how we can create value together - a big thank you from all of us. We cannot exist without you.

#### Platform Growth
The platform keeps growing every year to become more stable and capable.

One objective this year was to make the application easier to configure. Some new features to enable this include

* **An AI Assistant** - that helps users understand Avni and build applications using natural language. Currently available in beta, it can answer questions about the platform and create forms and some rules, with capabilities being expanded iteratively
* **Application Templates** - that address the starting point of development. Templates are production-ready starter applications that can be applied to an Avni account and then customised before going live. The platform now supports publishing, browsing, and applying templates
* **Out-of-the-box analytics** - to provide basic visualisations, prebuilt to ensure basic metrics to monitor the program are available right away

Other notable features that support end users include the ability to **associate images with questions and answers**, and richer **documentation through videos embedded in forms**.

The overall system is stable and faster with a **4X improvement in overall response times** through better infrastructure, and a **50% reduction in support requests**.

#### Increased Reach and Impact
In this year, we significantly increased our focus on outreach and simplifying our onboarding processes towards maximising the impact. We onboarded **13 new accounts** this year, better than any past year in Avni‚Äôs history of 9 years.

Apart from the regulars like health, education, water and social security, **agriculture, sports and legal aid** are the **new domains** where Avni got used in this year. Every new domain and use case enriches our knowledge and the underlying platform. Projects are a healthy mix of both service delivery and research.
While expanding into new domains, a significant part of our work this year was also about going deeper in existing ones.

* We continued improving Avni for **village-level health workers**, focusing on usability in low-connectivity settings and simpler workflows.
* We worked with NGOs taking health programs to **new geographies**, adapting implementations to local contexts and capacities.
* Our work in **social security** deepened further, emerging as a recurring theme with multiple projects approaching us in this domain.
* We also saw **multiple new projects around non-communicable diseases (NCDs)**, building on earlier health-related work and long-term data collection needs.
This combination of depth and breadth has helped Avni mature as a platform that supports both long-running programs and newer use cases.

#### Avni Launchpad
We started [Avni Launchpad](https://avniproject.org/blog/2025-10-30-avni-launchpad-announcement/), a cohort-based mentoring program. The program is designed to ease the decision making for NGOs wanting to adopt Avni. In a span of two months, with free mentoring and cloud credits, participating NGOs build their own pilot apps and experience Avni‚Äôs value firsthand. For the first cohort we received an excellent response with 49 applications. We onboarded 13 organisations. They will be testing their pilot until Jan 30th and then choose to move towards a full and paid rollout.

In a 2-day workshop held in Bangalore it was heartening to see how :
* Diverse use cases were being digitised using Avni under the same roof
* Focus and motivation of NGOs wanting to learn Avni and build their apps
* Team members step up from their roles of building software to listening to problem statements and mentor NGOs.

#### Ecosystem strengthening

With almost a decade long experience, we felt the time was right in stepping up our role towards sharing our learnings with the sector. We conducted **Field data collection workshops** across different cities and networks with our partners. As part of the workshop we got an opportunity to listen to challenges that NGOs are currently facing and share our learnings from digitising 60+ projects with diverse needs.

This year we joined as mentors on
* **C4GT** - a program to encourage ongoing contributions and strengthen collaboration between DPG/DPI builders, adopters, and contributors (students or working professionals)
* **Data Catalyst Program** - an initiative, for non-profits or social impact organizations, designed to build capacity for using data effectively to drive strategy, improve operations, and achieve greater outcomes, by fostering a data culture, improving skills, and implementing practical data solutions for real-world challenges
